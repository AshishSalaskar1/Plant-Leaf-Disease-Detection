Corn Common Rust

    Common rust of corn occurs every year to some extent, and is caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi.
    The diseases can increase in severity rapidly, particularly if infection occurs early in the growing season and favorable environmental conditions persist for extended periods of time. Timing of disease development and its severity determine the extent of yield loss.
    The most useful visual characteristic of common rust is the location of sporulating pustules (pustules where spores are produced). Common rust pustules frequently occur on both the upper and lower leaf surfaces. Pustules of the common rust fungus tend to be circular to elongate, darker in color, less densely scattered, and slightly larger. Pustules darken in color later in the season as the fungus produces teliospores instead of red urediniospores.
